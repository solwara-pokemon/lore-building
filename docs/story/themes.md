# Core Themes

## Primary Theme: Acceptance
The central theme of Solwara is the difference between **resignation** and **genuine acceptance**.

The player spends the entire game believing they are working toward restoring the world to its prior state. The climax reframes everything: the world is not broken. It changed. And the real antagonist of the story is the collective inability to let go.

> "There is no fixing this."

This is not a nihilistic message. The ending should feel **bittersweet but ultimately warm**. The world changed, people were lost, but look at what became of it — new life, new culture, new beauty. A eulogy that turns into a celebration.

## Secondary Theme: Collective Delusion
No one in this story is purely a villain. The Unforgotten Party acts in good faith. Their plan is impossible not because they are evil but because the truth — that Groudon is dead — has never been discovered. Everyone is pointed in an impossible direction with sincere hearts.

## Age Range & Tone
- **Target audience:** Teens to adults
- **Awareness:** Children will play it; content must remain accessible
- **Model:** Pixar approach — surface is fun and accessible, underlying themes land harder for older players
- **Limits:** Grief and loss are present but not graphic. Moral ambiguity without cynicism. Death implied, not shown.

## The Ending
The player discovers the truth through Kyogre itself. Not a battle — a vision. Kyogre shows what actually happened to Groudon. The player must then decide whether to tell The Unforgotten Party the truth and destroy their hope, or protect them from it.

The ending should feel earned. Acceptance is not defeat.
