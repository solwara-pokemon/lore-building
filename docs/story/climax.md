# The Kyogre Revelation

## Setup
The player has spent the entire game chasing Groudon rumors alongside — and sometimes against — The Unforgotten Party. The party believes they are close to a breakthrough. The player arrives first and finds not Groudon, but **Kyogre**.

## The Encounter
This is not a battle.

Kyogre does not attack. It has been waiting. It is the only living being that knows the truth, and it has carried that knowledge alone since The War while the world blamed it.

Kyogre shows the player a vision — the actual final moments of the conflict. Groudon falling. The waters rising. Kyogre, alone, surviving something it never asked to be part of.

## The Revelation
Groudon is dead. Has been since The War. The Unforgotten Party has been searching for something that no longer exists.

Kyogre is not a monster. It is the **only living witness to the truth** — and it has been alone with that truth while the world mourned through it.

## The Choice
The player must decide:
- **Tell The Unforgotten Party the truth** — destroy their hope, force acceptance
- **Protect them from it** — leave the delusion intact

This choice and its consequences are the emotional climax of the game. There is no clean answer.

## Why Kyogre
Kyogre as truth-teller is the most poetic possible resolution. The thing the world fears, blames, and cannot forgive is the only one who can set them free. The player has to go to it — not to fight, not to capture — just to listen.
