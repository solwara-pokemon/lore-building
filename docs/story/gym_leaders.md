# Gym Leaders

## Overview
8 gyms representing the 8 stages of grief. Each leader embodies a different relationship with the flood. Defeating a gym leader is not just a mechanical milestone — it is an emotional chapter.

Gyms follow a Slay the Spire style map structure. The player navigates nodes to reach the leader.

All gym leaders field 5 Pokémon, except Lena (6). No Mega Evolutions or Gigantamax forms permitted.

---

## Gym 1 — Shock & Denial
**Leader:** Jon | **Type:** Normal | **Badge:** The Denial Badge | **Level Range:** ~20

**Personality:** Warm, welcoming, genuinely kind. Has built an entire performance of pre-flood normalcy and convinced a community to perform it with him. Not sinister — heartbreaking.

**The Gym:** Looks like a bustling pre-flood city. Art depicting skyscrapers, volcanoes, vast plains. The closer you look the wronger it feels. Nobody acknowledges the ocean visible through the windows.

|---|---|

| Stufful | Looks harmless, hits harder than expected |
| Cinccino | Quick, polished, put-together exterior |
| Dudunsparce (Ace) | Changed but did not change. Denial in Pokémon form. |

## Gym 2 — Pain & Guilt
**Leader:** Reese (Female) | **Type:** Poison | **Badge:** The Painful Badge | **Level Range:** ~25-30

**Personality:** Quiet, exhausted in a way that goes bone deep. Has spent her life cataloguing the names of everyone lost in the flood. Thought documenting the pain would process it. Instead she is drowning in it. Not unkind — just carrying too much.

**The Gym:** Every surface covered in names. Floor, ceiling, walls — layers of handwriting in different colors, different ages of ink. Others have come to add names. It became a pilgrimage site without her intending it. She cannot leave now — she is the keeper of everyone elses pain.

**Team:**
| Pokémon | Notes |
|---|---|
| Mareanie | Absorbs toxicity quietly |
| Haunter | Lingers, cannot move on |
| Seviper | Carries venom it did not choose |
| Drapion (Ace) | Resilient, scarred, still standing |

## Gym 3 — Anger & Bargaining
**Leader:** Mari (Female) | **Type:** Fighting | **Badge:** The Anger Badge | **Level Range:** ~30-35

**Personality:** Inherited fury. Lost something specific and concrete through family history. The Unforgotten Partys loudest public voice in her region — not a formal member, just someone whose rage aligns with their goals. Does not waste words.

**The Gym:** Active, chaotic training ground. Constant noise, people sparring, no stillness anywhere. Stillness means thinking. Thinking means feeling. Architecture partially destroyed and rebuilt repeatedly.

**Team:**
| Pokémon | Notes |
|---|---|
| Primeape | Pure unfiltered rage |
| Bewear | Aggressive affection — love expressed as force |
| Passimian | Coordinated, team-focused anger |
| Slaking/Fighting type (Ace) | Rage...bargain...rage...bargain. Truant as grief mechanic. |

*Note: Slaking is hard set as Fighting type via type-changing node mechanic.*

## Gym 4 — Depression & Loneliness
**Leader:** Lupa (Male) | **Type:** Ice | **Badge:** The Lonely Badge | **Level Range:** ~35-40

**Personality:** Did not dramatically collapse — just slowly stopped. Keeps routines, maintains the gym, does what is expected. Gentle with the player. Speaks like someone reading from a script of how humans are supposed to interact. Nobody home behind the eyes.

**The Gym:** Vast, sparse, cold. Huge windows over open ocean. Long corridors with almost no trainers. The silence is architectural — Lupa designed it that way without realizing.

**Team:**
| Pokémon | Notes |
|---|---|
| Snorunt | Small, isolated, barely there |
| Beartic | Solitary, drifting, cold endurance |
| Cryogonal | No face. Just geometry. Alone by nature. |
| Glaceon (Ace) | Chose isolation. Froze itself into something beautiful and untouchable. |

## Gym 5 — The Upward Turn
**Leader:** Bobby (Female) | **Type:** Electric | **Badge:** The Turning Badge | **Level Range:** ~40-45

**Personality:** Got better without a grand narrative. One morning something felt different and she watered her plants. Then fixed a broken window. Then started talking to her neighbors. Small choices accumulating into something resembling living. Slightly awkward about her own recovery — feels she has not earned the lightness.

**The Gym:** Bright, slightly chaotic, visibly unfinished. Plants everywhere, some thriving, some recovering. Mismatched furniture. Evidence of someone redecorating mid-thought. First gym where actual genuine laughter is heard.

**Team:**

| Dedenne | Tiny, persistent, refuses to quit |
| Jolteon | Sharp, reactive, newly awake |
| Ampharos (Ace) | Steady warm light. A lighthouse. |

## Gym 6 — Reconstruction
**Leader:** Bram (Male) | **Type:** Steel/Rock | **Badge:** The Rebuilding Badge | **Level Range:** ~45-50

**Personality:** Grieved with his hands. The day after the worst moment of his life he picked up tools and started building. Has not really stopped since. His settlement is the most structurally impressive in Solwara. The question nobody asks — is he building because he believes in the future, or because stopping means feeling everything he has been outrunning?

**The Gym:** Active construction site. Literal scaffolding, materials everywhere, work happening around the player. Trainers are workers on break. The badge is earned in a room that was not finished when the player arrived.

**Team:**
| Pokémon | Notes |
|---|---|
| Boldore | Raw material, unrefined, powerful |
| Magneton | Precise, structural, holds things together |
| Steelix | Built from something ordinary into something unbreakable |
| Bastiodon | Immovable, protective, a wall against the past |
| Aggron (Ace) | Builds its own armor. Reconstructs itself constantly. |

## Gym 7 — Acceptance
**Leader:** Lena (Female) | **Type:** Water | **Badge:** The Accepted Badge | **Level Range:** ~50-55

**Personality:** Lost as much as anyone. Will tell you plainly if you ask — no drama, no performance. Does not remember exactly when it shifted. One day the ocean looked beautiful instead of cruel. People come from all over Solwara just to sit with someone who seems okay. She makes tea. She listens. She does not try to fix anything.

**The Gym:** The most beautiful location in the game. Open water all around, structures built with the ocean rather than against it. Light filtering through shallow water overhead. Bioluminescent Pokémon visible in the depths below glass floors. Completely, achingly still.

**Team:**
| Pokémon | Notes |
|---|---|
| Slowbro | Unbothered, deep, arrived somewhere long ago |
| Alomomola | Caretaker, heals naturally, gives without asking |
| Vaporeon | Dissolved into the water, became part of it |
| Primarina | Sings for the lost, keeps singing anyway |
| Milotic | Beauty found in turbulent origins |
| Suicune (Ace) | Purifies what it touches. Moves forward always. |

*Note: Suicune is a legendary — hard set at appropriate level.*

## Gym 8 — Hope & New Meaning
**Leader:** Sol (Male) | **Type:** Flying | **Badge:** The Hope Badge | **Level Range:** ~55-60

**Personality:** Grew up looking up. While everyone around him looked at what the water took, he watched the sky. Birds still fly. Clouds still move. The sun still rises every morning without fail. Found meaning in the things the flood could not touch. Not naive about the loss — just chose to look at what is not underwater.

**The Gym:** The most open, airy, breathtaking location in the game. All sky and light and soaring Pokémon.

**The Interruption:** The player never completes this gym in a normal run. The Unforgotten Party makes their move during Gym 8. The Hope Badge remains grayed out in the badge case for the entire climax — a constant reminder of what was interrupted.

**Unlocking Sol:** Fighting Sol becomes possible after beating the game with all three launch roles (Hero, The Doctor, The Accepted). The Hope Badge is the ultimate completion reward. The last thing in the badge case to light up.

| Talonflame (Ace) | Flies highest, burns brightest, never stops climbing |

## Rival: Evan
- **Gender:** Non-conforming
- **Starter:** Flamigo with Scrappy ability (hard set)
- **Background:** Native Solwarian, distant cousin of Archie (Team Aqua leader). Knows about the family connection. Carries generational guilt for something they did not do.
- **Relationship:** Never fully becomes the players friend but is not their enemy either. Disdain masks exhaustion. Respects the player eventually — grudgingly, unspoken.
- **Arc:** Spent their whole life defined by something they did not do. Journey is learning to carry the family history without being crushed by it.

*Note: This game takes place 100 years after Team Aqua. Archie is a historical figure to Evan, not a living relative.*
