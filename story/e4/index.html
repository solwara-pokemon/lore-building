    Elite Four & Final Boss

    Structure

    The E4 is not a linear gauntlet — it is a separate node map like each gym. E4 members are scattered within the nodes with no defined order. Any two can appear in any run in any sequence.

    The Interruption

    • After the 2nd E4 fight, each subsequent node has an escalating chance of interruption:
    • Next node after 2nd fight: 50%
    • Following node: 75%
    • Node after that: 100%
    • The interruption is a mysterious emergency requiring the players specific attention
    • The caller is unknown at the time — it is Kyogre
    • This leads directly into the climax sequence

    E4 Members

    Mark — Naval Commander

    • Gender: Male
    • Party relationship: Opportunist — supports whoever is politically advantageous
    • Ace: Garry (Gyarados) with Supreme Overlord
    • Pool flavor: Disciplined, naval, built for sustained combat. Water, Steel, Fighting adjacent.
    • Frenemy dynamic: Most outwardly composed. Quiet, precise digs. Devastating without raising his voice.

    Sally — Small Town Mayor/Diplomat

    • Gender: Non-conforming
    • Party relationship: Complicated neutral
    • Ace: Arceus with Libero
    • Pool flavor: No type restrictions. Approachable, resilient, dangerously underestimated.
    • Frenemy dynamic: The mediator. Knows where all the bodies are buried. Warm but never foolish.

    Tau — Scientist/Oceanographer

    • Gender: Male
    • Party relationship: Opponent — deals in evidence, finds the partys mythology dangerous
    • Ace: Aerodactyl with Storm Drain
    • Pool flavor: Research oriented, ancient, analytical. Fossil Pokémon, things that survived against the odds.
    • Frenemy dynamic: Argues constantly but always factually. Exhausting to Mark. Sally quietly agrees with him more than they admit.

    Jabari — School Teacher

    • Gender: Male
    • Party relationship: True believer — genuine, warm, deeply personal
    • Ace: Dialga with Quick Draw
    • Pool flavor: History, memory, preservation. Ancient Pokémon, things that carry the weight of time.
    • Frenemy dynamic: Most openly emotional. Tau and Jabari argue constantly. Sally loves him and worries about him.

    E4 Pool System

    Each member has a scripted ace plus 5 Pokémon drawn randomly from a pool of 25 per member. Fighting any E4 member twice never feels identical.

    Frenemy Dynamic

    The four E4 members are frenemies — people who have been in each others orbit so long they cannot tell if they actually like each other. Inspired by the relationship between Hiruluk and Kureha from One Piece. Genuine bond expressed almost entirely through antagonism and competitive one-upmanship.

    Level Range

    E4 battles: level 70-100. Hard cap for the player at this stage: 80.

    Final Boss — Evan

    Evan went with the player to Kyogre. They witnessed the same truth — Groudon is dead, restoration is impossible. And they cannot accept it.

    Evan fights because accepting the truth means accepting that their entire identity was built around an impossibility. They have spent their whole life defined by Archies name. The flood, the guilt, the generational shame. And now it was all for nothing.

    • Level: 100 (hard cap)
    • Starter: Flamigo with Scrappy (hard set)
    • Tone: This fight is to the death. Glazed over but hinted at. Dark enough to land, tasteful enough for the age range.

    Evan is not a villain. They are someone who could not stop running in time.
